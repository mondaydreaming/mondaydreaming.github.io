a little bit about me

I've loved creating since before I can remember. Growing up, I spent most of my time tinkering with various instruments, fabrics, paints, clays and the occasional soldering iron.

Somewhere along the line however, I developed a rebellious streak and ended up double majoring in accounting and finance. After school, I translated my addiction to learning and creating to a career designing business strategies.

Although I found the work rewarding, in my second last semester of university, I had taken a computer programming elective which planted a seed of interest with technology and the web that I just could not shake.

As it turned out, these were magic beans which grew into a giant beanstalk of fascination that took over the host body, culminating in the said host leaving their job to study web development full time.

The Web Development Immersive (Eight) at General Assembly in Sydney has cemented my passion for developing for the web - it is an incredible, living and breathing medium with the power to shape and be shaped by all who seek to participate. To me, it's pure magic. And how can you turn your back on magic once you've experienced it?
